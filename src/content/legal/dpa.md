## 1. Introduction

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the [Terms of Service](/terms) ("Agreement") between Lastest ("Processor", "we", "us", "our") and the customer entity that has accepted the Agreement ("Controller", "you", "your"). It governs the processing of personal data carried out by Lastest on behalf of the Controller in connection with the Lastest service ("the Service").

This DPA is intended to comply with Article 28 of the EU General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679, "GDPR") and, where applicable, the UK GDPR.

## 2. Definitions

Capitalized terms not defined here have the meanings given to them in the GDPR. In particular:

- **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person processed by Lastest on behalf of the Controller in connection with the Service.
- **"Data Subject"** means the individual to whom Personal Data relates.
- **"Processing"** means any operation performed on Personal Data, such as collection, storage, use, disclosure, or deletion.
- **"Sub-processor"** means a third party engaged by Lastest to process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

## 3. Roles of the Parties

The parties acknowledge that, in respect of Personal Data submitted to or generated through the Service by the Controller and its authorized users:

- The Controller is the **controller** of the Personal Data.
- Lastest is the **processor** of the Personal Data and processes it only on the Controller's documented instructions, as set out in the Agreement and this DPA.

## 4. Subject Matter and Details of Processing

- **Subject matter:** provision of the Lastest visual regression testing service.
- **Duration:** for the term of the Agreement and any post-termination period required to return or delete Personal Data under Section 12.
- **Nature and purpose:** hosting, processing, and displaying Personal Data so the Controller can operate visual regression tests, review results, and manage its team.
- **Categories of Personal Data:** account information (name, email, password hash or OAuth identifiers), usage data (IP address, browser metadata, pages visited), and any Personal Data that may be incidentally captured in test data (e.g., screenshots of authenticated application states).
- **Categories of Data Subjects:** the Controller's authorized users, end users of the Controller's applications who appear in test data, and any other individuals whose data the Controller chooses to submit to the Service.

## 5. Processor Obligations

Lastest will:

- Process Personal Data only on the documented instructions of the Controller (including the Agreement, this DPA, and the Controller's use of the Service), unless required to do so by applicable law, in which case Lastest will inform the Controller of that legal requirement before processing where permitted.
- Ensure that personnel authorized to process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations.
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures as set out in Section 8.
- Assist the Controller, taking into account the nature of the processing, with its obligations under Articles 32–36 GDPR (security, breach notification, impact assessments, and consultation with supervisory authorities).
- Make available all information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with Article 28 GDPR.

## 6. Sub-processors

The Controller authorizes Lastest to engage Sub-processors to support the Service, such as hosting, email delivery, and operational tooling. As described in Privacy Policy section 6, Lastest binds Sub-processors to data protection terms no less protective than those in this DPA.

Lastest will inform the Controller of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of Sub-processors with reasonable advance notice, giving the Controller the opportunity to object on reasonable data protection grounds. If the parties cannot resolve the objection, the Controller may terminate the affected portion of the Service.

## 7. International Data Transfers

Where Personal Data is transferred outside the European Economic Area, Lastest will ensure that an appropriate transfer mechanism is in place, such as the EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and, where applicable, the UK International Data Transfer Addendum, consistent with Privacy Policy section 7.

## 8. Security

Lastest will implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures designed to protect Personal Data against unauthorized or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, damage, alteration, or disclosure. These measures include, at a minimum:

- Encryption of Personal Data in transit and, where applicable, at rest
- Role-based access controls and authentication for personnel
- Logging and monitoring of access to production systems
- Regular review of security practices and dependency hygiene
- Backup and disaster recovery procedures appropriate to the Service

## 9. Assistance with Data Subject Requests

Taking into account the nature of the processing, Lastest will assist the Controller by appropriate technical and organizational measures, insofar as possible, to fulfill the Controller's obligation to respond to requests from Data Subjects exercising their rights under GDPR Chapter III (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection).

If Lastest receives a request directly from a Data Subject in respect of Personal Data processed on the Controller's behalf, Lastest will, where lawful, redirect the Data Subject to the Controller and notify the Controller without undue delay.

## 10. Personal Data Breach Notification

Lastest will notify the Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of a Personal Data breach affecting the Controller's Personal Data. The notification will include, to the extent then known, the nature of the breach, the categories and approximate volume of Personal Data and Data Subjects concerned, the likely consequences, and the measures taken or proposed to address the breach.

## 11. Audits

Lastest will make available to the Controller information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with Article 28 GDPR and will allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller, subject to reasonable confidentiality obligations and at the Controller's expense. The parties will agree in advance on the scope, timing, and methodology of any audit so as to minimize disruption to the Service.

## 12. Return or Deletion of Personal Data

On termination of the Agreement, Lastest will, at the Controller's choice, delete or return all Personal Data processed on the Controller's behalf, and delete existing copies, unless retention is required by applicable law. Default deletion timelines align with Privacy Policy section 9.

## 13. Liability

The liability of each party under this DPA is subject to the limitations of liability set out in the Agreement (see Terms of Service section 8), to the extent permitted by applicable law.

## 14. Governing Law

This DPA is governed by the same law as the Agreement, namely the laws of Hungary and, where applicable, the laws of the European Union (see Terms of Service section 10).

## 15. Order of Precedence

In the event of any conflict between this DPA and the Agreement, this DPA prevails with respect to matters relating to the processing of Personal Data.

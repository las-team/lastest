## 1. About This Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how Lastest ("we", "us", "our") uses cookies and similar technologies on the Lastest service ("the Service"). It should be read together with our [Privacy Policy](/privacy) and [Terms of Service](/terms).

## 2. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that a website places on your device when you visit it. They are widely used to make websites work or work more efficiently, and to provide information to the site's operators. Similar technologies include local storage and session storage in your browser.

## 3. Cookies We Use

We only use cookies and similar storage that are strictly necessary to operate the Service. Specifically:

- **Authentication session cookies:** set when you sign in, used to keep you signed in across pages and to protect your account from unauthorized access. Issued by our authentication layer (better-auth) on the same domain as the Service.
- **CSRF protection tokens:** used to protect form submissions and state-changing actions against cross-site request forgery.
- **Browser local/session storage:** used to remember UI preferences (e.g., theme, layout state) on your device. This data stays on your device and is not transmitted to us as a cookie.

These items are essential — disabling them will prevent core parts of the Service (sign-in, navigation, settings) from working.

## 4. No Third-Party Tracking or Advertising

We do **not** use third-party advertising cookies, cross-site tracking pixels, or behavioral profiling. We do not sell your personal data, and we do not allow advertising networks to track you through the Service. See Privacy Policy section 5 for details.

## 5. Analytics

If we use analytics, it is limited to first-party, privacy-respecting measurement of aggregate usage of the Service to help us improve performance and user experience. We do not combine analytics data with advertising profiles.

## 6. Managing Cookies in Your Browser

You can control or delete cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers let you:

- View the cookies stored on your device
- Block cookies from specific sites or all sites
- Delete cookies on exit
- Use private/incognito browsing modes

Because the cookies we use are strictly necessary, blocking them will likely cause parts of the Service to stop working — for example, you will not be able to stay signed in.

For browser-specific instructions, see your browser's help pages (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge, etc.).
